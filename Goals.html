- Fall Goals

Efficiently Read Scholarly Articles

Initially, I found reading scholarly articles time-consuming. One of my goals this term was to improve my efficiency. I discovered that reading in the following order—abstract, conclusion, graphs, introduction, discussion, and methods—worked best. This approach allows me to quickly understand the article's purpose and key findings, with graphs reinforcing the conclusions. If the article remains relevant and engaging, I delve deeper into the remaining sections for details on how the conclusions were reached.

Strengthen my Ability in Excel

As a data analyst, presenting data in a clear and organized way is crucial. Throughout this work term, I significantly improved my Excel skills to present my findings more effectively. I gained a deep understanding of Excel's programming language, enabling me to automate tasks such as locking specific cells and organizing multiple columns simultaneously. Additionally, I became proficient in using Excel's graphing tools and PivotTables for quick and insightful analysis.

Alternatives to PowerBI

During a major project, our team began using Power BI to visualize the results. However, I found this approach inefficient for handling the large datasets we were working with, as Power BI is better suited for smaller datasets. I proposed using R, which could graph the extensive dataset more efficiently and handle potential increases in size. This led to a division within the team: the IT team supported R, while the Project Managers preferred Power BI due to their familiarity with it. Ultimately, the team chose to stick with Power BI, but I believe the discussion highlighted R's capabilities and broadened the team's perspective.
